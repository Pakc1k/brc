Executive Summary

$BRC (Barong vs Rangda Coin) is a 30-year decentralized digital initiative built on dual-token mechanics and Balinese mythic imagery. Using Barong and Rangda as symbolic civic identities, the project enables a fully digital ecosystem focused on measurable, on-chain impact: Barong leads environmental missions (cleanups, awareness), while Rangda leads education and empowerment (especially women and children). All engagement for the first 10 years is digital.

1. Myth-Inspired Digital Infrastructure

Barong and Rangda are reinterpreted as digital archetypes:

  • Barong represents Order, Discipline, Environmental Care.
  • Rangda symbolizes Disruption, Learning, Human Empowerment.

These characters are not spiritual representations in this context. Their artistic forms are used respectfully as modern civic identities. Users align with a faction and complete missions online to earn tokens, relics, and reputation.

3. Technical Architecture

  • Phase 1: Ethereum L2, ERC-1155 relics, digital missions
  • Phase 2: Cosmos SDK, mission VM, proof-of-impact tokens
  • Phase 3: ZK-verification, AI lore bots
  • Phase 4–6: Modular consensus, interchain mesh, civic oracle layers

4. Token Lifecycle

$BRC (Utility Token): Digital mission engine, relic minting, validator rewards.

$RANGDA (Governance Token): Earned via social impact, grants proposal and voting.

  • 20% reserved for contributors (vested over 30 years)
  • 5% of fees fund cleanup and education
  • Impact-based token burns and deflation

5. Digital Impact Systems

  • Mission validation via pledges and digital tasks
  • Grants DAO for Bali-focused impact work
  • Soulbound NFTs for civic progress
  • Open source modules for other chains

6. Long-Term Vision

By 2055, $BRC will evolve into a fully autonomous civic coordination protocol — measuring and rewarding on-chain actions that generate real-world impact. Governance becomes trustless and symbolic dynamics (Barong/Rangda) serve as interfaces for collective action, not speculation. This project is not spiritual or religious; it is a symbolic, artistic, and decentralized response to modern civic challenges.
